Your Wedding Audio, Thought Through

These are the questions and moments worth thinking about early—so nothing gets missed on your big day.

Ceremony Audio

Every Moment Has a Soundtrack

Prelude

20–30 min before ceremony

Music as guests arrive and settle in. Sets the tone—romantic, relaxed, upbeat. Plan 5–10 songs that reflect you as a couple.

Processional

3–10 minutes

The walk down the aisle. Traditionally 2–3 pieces: family seating, wedding party, and the couple's entrance.

Ceremony

During the service

Unity ceremony music, readings with background audio, or featured songs during special moments. Crystal-clear mics for vows.

Recessional

3–4 minutes

The celebratory walk back! Upbeat, joyful, energy-filled. The first song you hear as a married couple—make it count.

Postlude

10–15 minutes

Music as guests exit and transition to cocktail hour. Keeps the celebratory energy going as everyone moves together.

Live vs. DJ

Consider both

Combining live ceremony music with DJ reception is popular. I coordinate seamlessly with live musicians of any kind.

Think It Through

Questions Worth Asking Yourselves

Venue & Setup

  • Indoors or outdoors? (outdoor = extra audio care)
  • Same space for ceremony and reception?
  • How large is the venue?
  • Any noise concerns nearby?
  • Separate cocktail hour space?

Timeline & Flow

  • How long will the reception be?
  • Cocktail hour with music?
  • Speeches during dinner or after?
  • How many speeches & how long?
  • Grand entrance announcement?

Music & Vibe

  • Genres you love? Must-not-plays?
  • First dance song picked out?
  • Parent dances?
  • Songs for cake, bouquet, last dance?
  • Non-stop party or relaxed with peaks?

Audio Needs

  • Wireless mics for vows & officiant?
  • Mic for toasts and speeches?
  • Background music during dinner?
  • Live performances needing a mic?
  • Ambient uplighting to set the mood?
Pro Tips

Things Most Couples
Don't Think About

Outdoor Ceremony? Plan for Sound.

Wind, open air, and ambient noise can swallow audio. Professional wireless mics and placed speakers are essential—not optional.

Speeches Need Mics. Always.

Even in small venues. A wireless mic ensures everyone hears every word and prevents the "speak up!" interruptions.

Energy Has a Shape

The best receptions build gradually—ambient during dinner, rising through speeches, peaking on the dance floor.

Finalize Details 2+ Weeks Out

Song choices, timeline, announcements—lock them in early so your DJ can prepare and rehearse transitions perfectly.
