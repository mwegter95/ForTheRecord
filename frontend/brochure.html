    Planning Workshop
    Your Wedding Audio, Thought Through

    These are the questions and moments worth thinking about early—so nothing gets missed on your big day. Use this as a guide as you plan, and we'll work through the details together.

    Ceremony Audio
    Every Moment Has a Soundtrack

    Prelude

    20–30 min before ceremony

    Music as guests arrive and settle in. Sets the tone—romantic, relaxed, upbeat. Plan 5–10 songs that reflect you as a couple.

    Processional

    3–10 minutes

    The walk down the aisle. Traditionally 2–3 pieces: one for family seating, one for the wedding party, and one for the couple's entrance.

    Ceremony

    During the service

    Unity ceremony music, readings with background audio, or featured songs during special moments. Crystal-clear microphones for vows and readings.

    Recessional

    3–4 minutes

    The celebratory walk back! Upbeat, joyful, energy-filled. This is the first song you hear as a married couple—make it count.

    Postlude

    10–15 minutes

    Music as guests exit and transition. Keeps the celebratory energy going as everyone moves to cocktail hour or the reception space.

    Live vs. DJ

    Consider both

    Will any parts feature a live musician? Combining live ceremony music with DJ reception is a popular option. I coordinate seamlessly with live musicians.

    Think It Through
    Questions Worth Asking Yourselves

    Venue & Setup

    • Is our ceremony indoors or outdoors? (outdoor = extra audio care)
    • Are the ceremony and reception in the same space or different?
    • How large is the venue? Will guests in the back row hear clearly?
    • Are there noise concerns nearby? (roads, other events, nature)
    • Is there a separate cocktail hour space that needs music?

    Timeline & Flow

    • How long will our reception be? (typical: 4–5 hours)
    • Will there be a cocktail hour, and would we like music there?
    • When do we want speeches/toasts? During dinner or after?
    • How many speeches, and how long? (budget ~5 min each)
    • Do we want a grand entrance announcement?

    Music & Vibe

    • What genres do we love? What should absolutely NOT be played?
    • Do we have a first dance song picked out?
    • Parent dances? Father-daughter, mother-son, or both?
    • Any special songs for cake cutting, bouquet toss, or last dance?
    • What energy do we want? Non-stop party or relaxed with peaks?

    Audio Needs

    • Do we need wireless mics for vows and officiant?
    • Will there be a mic for toasts and speeches?
    • Do we want background music during dinner?
    • Any readings, poems, or live performances that need a mic?
    • Do we want ambient uplighting to set the mood?
    Pro Tips
    Things Most Couples Don't Think About

    Outdoor Ceremony? Plan for Sound.

    Wind, open air, and ambient noise can swallow audio. Professional wireless mics and speakers positioned for your specific space are essential—not optional—outdoors.

    Speeches Need Mics. Always.

    Even in small venues. A wireless mic ensures Grandma in the back hears every word of the best man's toast—and prevents the "can you speak up?" interruptions.

    Energy Has a Shape

    The best receptions build energy gradually—ambient during dinner, rising through speeches, peaking on the dance floor. Your DJ should read the room, not just press play.

    Finalize Details 2+ Weeks Out

    Song choices, timeline, special announcements—lock these in at least two weeks before so your DJ can prepare, download, and rehearse transitions perfectly.
